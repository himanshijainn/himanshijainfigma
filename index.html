⬚ About — In detail

Who I am

I'm a UI/UX designer three-plus years into building scalable, data-driven products. My route in was unusual: I started out writing backend code, and five months in I realised the part that pulled at me wasn't the system itself but the narrow space where it meets the person using it. I still think in components and constraints — I just point that engineering habit at human problems now.

These days I own design end-to-end at Dancing Numbers: research, information architecture, flows, high-fidelity screens, design QA, and the system that keeps it all consistent. It's a QuickBooks automation SaaS that 50,000+ accountants across the US and Canada depend on to get through their day.

What I do

My home turf is SaaS and dense, data-first interfaces — the screens people open with a job to finish, not time to kill. That calls for tight flows, defaults that do the thinking, and layouts that don't waste a second of anyone's attention. I stay close to PMs and engineers, prototype early so the hard trade-offs surface while they're still cheap, and I treat empty states as first-class screens, not afterthoughts.

A few results I'm proud of: 25% fewer onboarding drop-offs after reworking the flow · 15% more daily active users following a dashboard redesign · 20% less hand-off friction once I rebuilt the team's component library.

Away from the screen

Off the clock I'm usually chasing a flat white worth the detour, reorganising my Figma libraries for the umpteenth time, or watching the hills roll past from a slow train. I keep a quiet collection of small interface details done right — receipts, metro cards, museum wayfinding — and pick them apart the way some people rewatch their favourite films.
