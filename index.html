⬚ About — In detail

Who I am

I'm a UX designer with 3+ years of experience building scalable, data-driven products. I came into design through code — I spent five months as a backend developer before realising the part I loved was the seam between the system and the human using it. That dual lens shows up in my work: I think in components, but I design for the messy edges where real people live.

Today I lead end-to-end design at Dancing Numbers — research, IA, flows, hi-fi, design QA, and the design system that holds it all together — on a QuickBooks automation SaaS trusted by 50,000+ accountants across the USA and Canada.

What I do

I design for SaaS and data-heavy interfaces — products where users come to get a job done. That means tight workflows, sensible defaults, and screens that respect your attention. I work closely with PMs and engineers, prototype to make trade-offs visible early, and obsess about empty states.

Recent wins: reduced onboarding drop-offs 25% through flow optimisation · lifted daily active users 15% with a dashboard redesign · cut design hand-off friction 20% by rebuilding the team's component library.

Off the clock

You'll find me hunting for coffee shops that get the foam right, re-organising my Figma libraries for the third time this week, or on a slow train somewhere in the hills. I keep a running list of tiny UX details I love — receipts, transit cards, museum signage — and study them like other people study films.
